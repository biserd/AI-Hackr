You are improving AIHackr, a SaaS AI-stack intelligence product. The current report page feels too much like a raw diagnostic receipt and not enough like a paid business intelligence report. Redesign the report experience so users feel it answers a valuable question: “What can I learn about this SaaS’s AI strategy, and what should I do next?”

Goal:
Turn each AIHackr scan report into a clear, paid-worthy “AI Intelligence Report” that interprets findings, explains unknowns, recommends next actions, and cross-promotes LLM Margin where relevant.

Context:
AIHackr scans SaaS websites and attempts to detect:
- AI provider
- Model family
- AI gateway
- Streaming/WebSocket usage
- Chat UI
- Framework
- CDN/edge
- Analytics
- Evidence signals
- Confidence levels

Example current report for aitracker.run:
- AI Provider: No public signal
- Framework: Unknown
- Origin inferred: Unknown behind Cloudflare
- Services: 0 detected
- Overall confidence: Medium
- Tech stack: Cloudflare, Google Analytics GA4
- AI Stack: Provider no public signal, Gateway not observed, Streaming not observed, Chat UI not found
- Evidence: Cloudflare headers, GA4 scripts
- CTA buttons: Run AI Probe, Compare vs competitor, Add to Watchlist, Copy link, Rescan

Problem:
When no AI provider is detected, the report feels like it failed. “No public signal” feels low value, especially if the site markets itself as AI-powered. We need to make unknowns useful by explaining likely reasons, what was tested, what was not found, and what the user should do next.

Primary product positioning:
AIHackr should feel like “competitive intelligence for AI-native SaaS,” not just a tech-stack scanner.

New report structure:
1. AI Stack Verdict
2. Executive Summary
3. What We Found
4. What We Did Not Find
5. AI Stack Details
6. Detection Evidence
7. Recommended Next Actions
8. Competitive Comparison CTA
9. LLM Margin CTA
10. Commodity Tech Stack section

Design/UX requirements:
- Put an “AI Stack Verdict” card at the top of the report, above the metric cards.
- Make the AI verdict visually dominant.
- Move commodity tech stack findings like Cloudflare and GA4 lower on the page.
- Do not make Cloudflare/GA4 feel like the main result.
- Use plain-English interpretation beside technical findings.
- Unknown states should feel useful, not empty.
- Add badges such as:
  - Browser-visible AI not found
  - Server-side AI likely
  - Behind-login AI possible
  - Origin protected by Cloudflare
  - Probe recommended
- Add a clear “Best next action” field.

Verdict logic:
If no AI provider is detected but the site appears to market itself as AI-powered, show:
Title:
“AI likely runs server-side or behind login”

Description:
“We did not observe browser-visible calls to OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, Cohere, Azure OpenAI, Bedrock, or common AI gateways during this scan. This does not mean the app has no AI. It usually means AI calls are handled server-side, protected behind authentication, proxied through the app backend, or triggered only after deeper user interaction.”

Best next action:
“Run an AI Probe on the app’s core AI flow or scan an authenticated session.”

If no AI provider is detected and there is no obvious AI positioning:
Title:
“No browser-visible AI stack detected”

Description:
“We did not find public AI provider, model, gateway, streaming, or chat UI signals during this scan. This site may not use AI, or AI features may be hidden behind login or backend APIs.”

Best next action:
“Run an AI Probe if the product claims to have AI features.”

If provider is detected:
Title:
“AI provider detected: [Provider]”

Description:
“We observed public signals suggesting this SaaS uses [Provider], with [confidence] confidence. Review the evidence trail below and compare this stack against competitors.”

Best next action:
“Compare this SaaS against competitors or estimate model cost impact in LLM Margin.”

Executive Summary:
Generate a short summary with:
- One sentence verdict
- Confidence explanation
- What matters
- Recommended next step

Example for aitracker.run:
“AIHackr did not detect browser-visible AI provider calls on aitracker.run, even though the product appears to market AI coaching features. The most likely explanation is that AI calls happen server-side, behind login, or through protected backend APIs. Cloudflare and GA4 were detected, but no OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, gateway, streaming, or chat UI signals were observed. Run an AI Probe on the core coaching or analysis flow to test deeper interactions.”

What We Found section:
Show positive findings in human language.
Example:
- “Cloudflare edge protection detected”
- “Google Analytics GA4 detected”
- “Public AI provider calls were not visible”
- “No browser-side streaming transport observed”
- “No chat UI signature found during scan”

What We Did Not Find section:
Show missing AI signals explicitly.
Example:
- “No OpenAI browser calls”
- “No Anthropic browser calls”
- “No Google Gemini browser calls”
- “No Mistral/Cohere browser calls”
- “No Vercel AI SDK/LangChain/LlamaIndex frontend signature”
- “No AI gateway endpoint observed”
- “No SSE/WebSocket streaming signal observed”

AI Stack Details:
Keep the existing structured cards, but improve wording:
- Replace “No public signal” with “No browser-visible AI provider found”
- Replace “Framework Unknown” with “Frontend framework not fingerprinted”
- Replace “Origin Unknown behind Cloudflare” with “Origin protected by Cloudflare”
- Replace “0 services detected” with “No third-party AI/API services observed”
- Replace “Chat UI Not found” with “No public chat UI detected”

Each unknown item must include a tooltip or explainer:
“This means AI may still be running server-side, behind login, or through backend APIs. Public scans cannot see private server-to-server calls.”

Detection Evidence:
Improve evidence presentation:
- Group evidence into:
  - Headers
  - Scripts
  - Network calls
  - HTML patterns
  - DNS/hosting
  - AI-specific signals
- If evidence is commodity tech only, label it clearly:
  “Supporting tech-stack evidence, not AI-provider evidence.”
- Add a sentence:
  “These signals explain why we detected Cloudflare and GA4, but they do not confirm the underlying AI provider.”

Recommended Next Actions:
Add a card with 3 to 5 context-aware recommendations.

For hidden/unknown AI stack:
1. “Run AI Probe on the product’s core AI interaction.”
2. “Scan an authenticated session if AI features require login.”
3. “Compare against 3 similar AI products.”
4. “Model possible provider-cost scenarios in LLM Margin.”
5. “Add to Watchlist to detect future stack changes.”

For detected provider:
1. “Compare this provider choice against competitors.”
2. “Estimate cost-per-user in LLM Margin.”
3. “Add to Watchlist for model/provider migration alerts.”
4. “Export the report for internal competitive analysis.”

Competitive Comparison CTA:
Make this prominent.
Title:
“Compare this SaaS against competitors”

Copy:
“A single scan tells you what we found. A comparison shows whether this company’s AI stack is cheaper, more advanced, more protected, or more exposed than similar products.”

Button:
“Compare competitors”

LLM Margin integration:
Add a CTA block called “Estimate AI cost impact.”

If provider/model detected:
Copy:
“Detected provider signals can be used to estimate the margin impact of this AI stack. Model cost-per-user, gross margin, and breakeven MAU in LLM Margin.”

Button:
“Estimate margin impact”

Link format:
https://llmmargin.com/?utm_source=aihackr&utm_medium=scan_report&utm_campaign=stack_to_margin&provider=[provider]&model=[model]&domain=[scanned_domain]

If provider/model unknown:
Copy:
“Provider hidden? Model common OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, and open-source scenarios in LLM Margin to understand possible cost exposure.”

Button:
“Model cost scenarios”

Link format:
https://llmmargin.com/?utm_source=aihackr&utm_medium=scan_report&utm_campaign=unknown_stack_to_margin&domain=[scanned_domain]

Paid conversion improvements:
Add report teasers for paid features:
- “Run authenticated probe”
- “Monitor this SaaS”
- “Compare competitors”
- “Export AI intelligence report”
- “Get notified when this AI stack changes”

Do not hide the whole report behind a paywall. Instead:
- Free users see passive scan, verdict, basic evidence.
- Paid users get probe scans, comparisons, watchlists, exports, change history, and deeper evidence.

Suggested pricing/product framing:
Free:
- Passive scan
- Basic AI verdict
- Basic evidence

Pro:
- AI Probe
- Competitor comparison
- Watchlist
- Change alerts
- Exportable reports

Agency:
- Bulk scans
- White-label reports
- Client workspaces
- CSV/API export

Tone:
Use confident, plain-English, intelligence-report language.
Avoid making the report sound uncertain or apologetic.
Use “not observed” when technically accurate, but always explain what that means.

Bad:
“No public signal”

Better:
“No browser-visible AI provider found. AI may still be running server-side, behind login, or through protected backend APIs.”

Bad:
“0 services detected”

Better:
“No third-party AI/API services observed during public scan.”

Bad:
“Unknown”

Better:
“Not fingerprinted from public signals.”

Acceptance criteria:
- Report has a clear AI Stack Verdict at the top.
- Unknown/no-signal reports feel useful and actionable.
- Cloudflare and GA4 no longer dominate the perceived value of the report.
- User can immediately understand:
  - what was found
  - what was not found
  - what it means
  - what to do next
- LLM Margin is linked contextually from the scan report.
- Compare/watchlist/probe actions are positioned as the main paid upgrade paths.
- Existing technical evidence remains available, but is wrapped in interpretation.
- Mobile and desktop layouts remain clean and readable.

Please implement this as a polished SaaS report page, preserving the existing AIHackr visual style but making the report feel more like premium competitive intelligence.
